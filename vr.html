VR AND LIVE SPORTS


Spring Semester 2021

Design Oppurtunity

As the world evolves to future immersion technology, how can people experience these technologies in new ways without leaving the comfort of their own home? VR has the opportunity to evolve how TV and entertainment is viewed. Rather than sitting on the couch and watching what is being broadcasted, how can this experience be enhanced and personalized to the viewer? VR technology allows for people to feel like they are in the event and participating and can even bring new social interactions that wouldn’t happen otherwise.

The Solution

Bring VR technology into TV to allow their users to feel like they are attending a sporting event and can have a social experience from the comfort of their own home. I want to design a VR prototype that combines the VR experience and a live sporting event where the viewer is “sitting in the stands” surrounded by other VR members, family, friends, or strangers.

Learning Objectives

  • Analyze VR technologies and how it can fit within a live streaming service
  • Demonstrate the many applications of VR technology merged with Live TV through storyboarding
  • Create Personas based on the type of users found in research and storyboarding accompanied with a service blueprint/journey map to illustrate those persona’s interaction between VR and TV.
  • Produce numerous rapid sketching sessions to inform new insights and generated concepts

USER STORY

Meet Jason & Mark

Jason and Mark are long-time friends who met at a Tampa Bay Buccaneers game

Because of the COVID pandemic, Jason and mark haven’t attended a football game in over a year, and they have been left to sit on the couch and watch at home.
Both of them have grown very tired of this and often communicate to each other through a phone call during the game. They wish there was a solution for them to still enjoy the in-person feeling while still being within the safety of their own home.

Jason & Mark's Current Model

Jason and Mark have grown quite annoyed by not being able to share the game experience together. They love to be in the stadium and sharing an experience with each other. They like to be part of the spectacle, not a spectator.

How can their connection still be fostered even though they live 3 hours apart?

SOLUTION WALKTHROUGH

Jason realizes that there is a Buccaneers game at 3:00 and he wants to watch it with Mark. He unlocks his phone and opens the Watch Party Planning app.

Jason navigates to the friends list to make sure that Mark is online.
He's curious to what Mark has been up to, so he clicks on Mark's name under the friends list to check his feed
After seeing that Mark has been keeping up with all of the Buccaneer's games, he wants to host a watch party with some of his friends.

Before he does this though, Jason navigates to adding some friends in case they want to join.
Jason looks through some of his Facebook friends that also watch sports in VR and can see what sports they like to watch.

Jason adds Andrew Parker as a friend and then navigates to starting a watch party.
Jason starts the process to Host a Watch Party and enters the Lobby name, sets a passowrd, and makes the lobby Invite Only

Jason clicks Next and heads to the next step
Now Jason will add some of his friends and simultaneously send them invites. He invites Mark, his new friends Andrew, and another friend Dylan.
Mark and others then recieve a notification that Jason has invited them to a watch party. Here, they can accept, decline, or ignore the invite. If ignored, their invite will still be accessible through the notifications in the side menu of the app.
After Jason has sent out the invites, he now selects which sport they would like to watch. Because they are watching the Buccaneers game, he selects NFL
Jason is the greeted with all of the games on for the day. He selects the day's Buccaneers game against the Indianapolis Colts and can see that he selected the right game at the bottom-center of the screen.

He then clicks next to see the overview of the Watch Party
Jason can now see his Watch Party's overview. This is where all of his selections will appear. Such as the lobby name, password, lobby visibility, the guests he invited, and the game they will be watching.

He then clicks START and begins the Watch Party.
Jason then puts on his VR headset and is instantly transferred to the live game experience. He can see all his friends in avatar form and can see the banner at the top of his vision as he would typically see on TV. Jason, Mark, Dylan, and Andrew are so happy to share this game together and have a good time communicating with one another in VR while sharing this experience. Overall, Jason is happy that he can enjoy the football games again with Mark and others while still being in the safety of his own home and not watching on the TV anymore!

PROJECT SUMMARY

I had a wonderful time with this project. I enjoyed the independence and letting myself reach outside the bounds of what I was used to. At the beginning of this project, I wasn’t expecting it to be as research-heavy as it was but I thought it best to completely understand the space and create some concepts to back up the research I conducted.

Although I struggled with the project’s identity after the research phase, I had a lot of help from my peers and professors that allowed me to better see where I wanted this project to end. Making the decision to create a cross-channel experience greatly impacted the meaningfulness of the end product in a positive way. Although this decision was not made until the very end, I am still very happy with what I produced.

For future development of this project, I would like to focus on the actual VR experience rather than the planning stages. Because of the short time frame, I had to focus on only one of the two.

Overall, I very much enjoyed this project and am very happy with what I produced. It allowed me to see that I enjoy the research-side of UX much more than I thought and that although I worked alone on this project, I can manage my time very well and mitigate stress much better.
