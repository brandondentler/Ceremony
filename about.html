What does UX Mean to Me?

Designing with Empathy for the User

The definition of UX varies between who you ask. There is no definite definition. With that being said, across the board, designers describe UX as designing for the user's experience (hence the name).

So how would I define UX design and the experience it includes?

Design is not something that we are born into. It is not something we are immediately good at. It takes years of experience, knowledge and engagement to develop the skills. It is about maturity. Not just mental maturity or age, but in the social and cultural realm.

The Nature of Design

UX Design is about designing a specific experience that is empathetically-driven. It takes into account not only the aesthetic or function, but the emotions of delight and ease-of-use. It's not about the process, but the journey and the emotions someone feels along that journey.

Knowledge Is Key

If there is one thing that I would have never expected from myself, it is my willingness and eagerness to read design books. Whether these are assigned through class or personal preference, so much can be learned.




Design is like a bookcase. Gaining knowledge of design is gained one step (or book) at a time. And it isn't until you can step back and view the entirety of the "bookcase" that you can realize how far you've come. It isn't about a handful of activites or core elements, but the library of knowledge you can access.
